Thomas Bayes (1701 1761) was an English theologian and mathematician that belonged to the Royal Society (the oldest national scientific society in the world and the leading national organisation for the promotion of scientific research in Britain), where other eminent individuals have enrolled, like Newton, Darwin or Faraday. He developed one of the most important theorems of probability, which coined his name: Bayes Theorem, or the Theorem of Conditional Probability.

H - Hypothesis : Babubali is a great warrior (Existing belief) (Prior Belief)
E - Event/Evidence : Bahubali winnning the battle against enemy
P(H) - Probability of Babubali is a great warrior that is already known
P(E) - Probability of Babubali winning the battle against enemy
P(H|E) - Probalility of Babubali is a great warrior given that Babubali wins the battle. This means the new probability of Bahubali being a great warrior after observing an event or evidence. (New Belief) (Posterior Belief)
P(E|H) - Probability of Babubali winning the battle against enemy given that Bahubali is a great warrior


P(E) = P(H) P(E|H) + P(~H) P(E|~H)
P(E) is the complete Probability of Babubali winning the battle against enemy, no matter whether Bahubali is great warrior or not. It might be possible that Bahubali wins the battle by luck.
